"Futura, a typeface by German typeface designer, Paul Renner, was released in 1927 (Futura). Futura is a geometric sans-serif typeface, which means it is based on geometric shapes. Originally designed as a contribution to The New Frankfurt housing project in Germany, Futura is heavily influenced by Bauhaus deisgn. Futura is unique because it avoids decorative and non-essential elements."
"The letterforms are very balanced."
"For example, every stem is a rectangle or straight line and all letters have the same stroke weight (Typeface)."
"I chose to highlight the character “K” in Futura Condensed. For many typefaces, the “K” has one diagonal arm connected to the stem and a diagonal leg connected to the arm. The “K” is unique in Futura Condensed because the two diagonals come together to meet the vertical stem in the middle."
