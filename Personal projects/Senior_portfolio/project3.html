Grid Poster

Type Grid Convention

Design an 11" x 17" typographic poster promoting the hypothetical 2019 Type Grid Convention at the University of Notre Dame.

Tools

InDesign


“What are the limits of readability moving from highly structured to expressive playfulness?”


Problem

There are three main grid styles: Orthogonal, Diagonal and Freeform. Each style has its own strengths and weaknesses.


Solution

For maximum readability, create an orthogonal/grid poster. This consists of a composition consisting of flush-left typographic elements arranged within a grid structure. The right angled blocks of text parallel the right angle created by the horizontal and vertical edges of the page.

Ideation

When developing ideas for the design of this poster, I was drawn to both orthogonal and diagonal grid styles.

I decided to redesign this poster because it felt like "2019" and "Grid" were too far away and in the original version and that it was difficult to read. The new version includes the large "TYPE" but also keeps the title of the conference together so that it is easier to read.
